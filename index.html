Most product failures don't begin in code. They begin in interpretation. If two capable people can read the same requirement and imagine different outcomes, the spec already contains delivery risk. This product moves that conversation before engineering breakdown.

Architecture

Two layers. Deterministic first, AI second.

The workflow runs rule-based ambiguity detection before any AI call. Low-signal documents are short-circuited without wasting tokens.

spec-ambiguity-detector.json — node flow
Webhook
  └─▶ Provider Config
      └─▶ Normalize Spec Input
            └─▶ Extract Structural Signals  ← no AI
                  └─▶ Detect Deterministic Ambiguity Flags  ← no AI, vague term scan
                        └─▶ Validate Signal Strength
                              ├─▶ [Low Signal] → Low Signal Response  ← skips AI entirely
                              └─▶ [Valid] → Prep Spec Analysis Request
                                            └─▶ AI: Analyze Spec Ambiguity  ← AI call here
                                                    └─▶ Parse Spec Analysis
                                                            └─▶ Deterministic Quality Checks  ← post-AI validation
                                                                    └─▶ Human Review Override
                                                                            ├─▶ [portfolio] → AI: Portfolio Review  ← second AI call
                                                                            └─▶ [single] → Final Response  ← strips _debug_ fields
                                                                                                    └─▶ Respond to Webhook
Pattern Classification

10 canonical ambiguity categories

Every ambiguity finding is classified into one of these named, enumerated categories — consistent across prompts, schemas, and workflow code.

🌫️
vague_language
Terms like "fast," "robust," "seamless," or "intuitive" without measurable criteria.
🎯
missing_acceptance_criteria
Requirements with no verifiable success conditions. If you can't test it, it doesn't count.
👤
undefined_ownership
No named role, team, squad, or service assigned to a requirement, decision, or edge case.
❓
missing_decision_point
Feature direction described without documenting the unresolved choices or tradeoffs still open.
🗺️
scope_boundary_gap
No definition of what is excluded, deferred, or out of scope for this release.
🔗
dependency_blind_spot
Quiet reliance on external APIs, legal review, design assets, or approvals without naming them.
🧪
untestable_requirement
Requirements stated as intent or feeling rather than observable, measurable behavior.
⚡
contradictory_requirement
Two or more statements in the document that cannot both be true or that conflict on scope.
🏗️
assumption_without_validation
Unvalidated premises presented as established facts — market assumptions, user behaviors, system capabilities.
📄
low_signal_noise
Document too short or abstract for reliable analysis. Short-circuits before AI call.
Positioning

What this is and what it is not

The product has a narrow, specific purpose. It is not a general AI tool, a summarizer, or a spec writer.

✓ This is
✓A requirements-quality diagnostic tool
✓An ambiguity pattern classifier with 10 named categories
✓A structured diagnostic you can route to Slack, Notion, or Jira
✓A pre-build audit automation with portfolio review mode
✓Deterministic + AI, with hallucination checks on the AI output
✗ This is not
✗A spec writer or content generator
✗A document summarizer or compressor
✗A project management or ticketing tool
✗A legal or compliance reviewer
✗A replacement for product judgment
Example Output

Structured diagnostic, not prose advice

Every field is named, typed, and designed to be routed downstream. No "improve clarity" — only specific, actionable findings.

Audience

Who benefits from this

Anyone who touches a spec before engineering starts.

📋
Product Managers
Catch weak requirements before engineering handoff. PRDs are the source of truth — ambiguity here is expensive.
⚙️
Engineers
Get clearer implementation inputs and fewer assumption-driven builds. Know what you're building before you build it.
🎨
Designers
Surface missing flow logic, edge cases, and interaction assumptions before they become late-stage surprises.
🧪
QA Engineers
Get testable acceptance criteria instead of broad intent statements. Write tests against requirements, not assumptions.
🚀
Founders & Small Teams
Move fast without skipping clarity. Surfacing open questions and assumptions early is how fast teams stay aligned.
